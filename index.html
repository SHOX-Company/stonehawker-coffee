Where it all begins

A lake ringed by
sleeping volcanoes.

Lake Atitlán · Sierra Madre · Guatemala

More than a decade ago, our founder traveled to the Guatemalan highlands chasing a single question: where does the world's most complex coffee actually come from? The answer was a lake ringed by three ancient volcanoes, sitting at 5,280 feet above sea level in the cloud forests of the Sierra Madre.

What he found wasn't just extraordinary coffee. He found extraordinary people — Maya families farming the same volcanic hillsides their ancestors worked for generations, with a reverence for the land that no certification can manufacture.

Stonehawker was born from that first handshake, and built on every one that followed. Ten harvests later, we return to the same farms, pay above-market rates, and bring their work to American coffee drinkers who can finally taste the difference that soil — real, ancient, alive soil — makes in a cup.

The Science of Soil

Volcanic ash isn't a
nice detail. It's everything.

Mineral Density

Basalt from ancient eruptions breaks down over millennia into soil with extraordinary potassium, phosphorus, and calcium — the precise nutrients driving complex flavor development in coffee. No supplement replicates it.

Shade & Altitude

At 5,280ft in highland jungle, our beans grow slowly under a natural canopy. Slow development = lower acidity, higher sugar content, and layered complexity. American sun-grown coffee simply cannot compete.

Zero Inputs

The volcanic soil is so naturally rich that pesticides and synthetic fertilizers aren't just unnecessary — they'd be counterproductive. Our "organic" certification is the land itself, ten thousand years in the making.

Farm to Your Door

Five intentional steps.
No shortcuts.

01

Hand-Selected Harvest

Ripe cherries only — picked by hand on the volcanic slopes, sorted the same day. Machines don't work here. The hillsides don't allow it, and the quality demands they don't.

02

Washed & Sun-Dried

Beans are washed in fresh mountain spring water and dried under the Guatemalan sun on raised African beds — even drying, no contact with the earth, clean cup.

03

Direct Import

We ship direct from farm to our US facility — no brokers, no warehousing middlemen. Green beans arrive fresh-crop and are roasted within weeks of clearing customs.

04

Small-Batch Roasting

Roasted in small batches to three precise profiles — Lago, Quetzal, Xocomil — each calibrated to what the bean's natural character is asking for. Never forced. Never masked.

05

Sealed & Shipped

Nitrogen-flushed, resealable bags heat-sealed within 24 hours of roasting. Delivered anywhere in the US. From volcanic soil to your cup in days, not months.
